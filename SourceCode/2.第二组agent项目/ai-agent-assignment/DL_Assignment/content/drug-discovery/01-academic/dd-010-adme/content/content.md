# ADME Properties

**Knowledge set ID:** `dd-010-adme`  
**Theme:** `drug-discovery`  
**Source:** SwissADME: a free web tool to evaluate pharmacokinetics, drug-likeness and medicinal chemistry friendliness  

## Curated Content

ADME stands for absorption, distribution, metabolism, and excretion. These properties determine how
a compound enters the body, reaches tissues, is chemically transformed, and leaves the body. ADME
problems can prevent a potent compound from becoming a useful drug because the compound may not
reach the target at sufficient exposure or may be cleared too quickly.  Important ADME measurements
include aqueous solubility, permeability, plasma protein binding, metabolic stability, cytochrome
P450 interactions, transporter effects, clearance, and bioavailability. Computational ADME
predictors help prioritize compounds, but predictions must be interpreted with experimental context
and applicability domains.  A correct Q&A answer should explain that ADME is about drug-like
behavior in biological systems, not simply target binding. Strong discovery programs consider ADME
early to avoid optimizing potency while ignoring exposure and safety.
