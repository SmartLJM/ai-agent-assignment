# Knowledge Graphs for Drug Discovery

**Knowledge set ID:** `dd-024-knowledge-graphs`  
**Theme:** `drug-discovery`  
**Source:** A review of biomedical datasets relating to drug discovery: a knowledge graph perspective  

## Curated Content

Knowledge graphs represent entities such as drugs, targets, diseases, pathways, side effects, genes,
and publications as nodes connected by typed relationships. In drug discovery, knowledge graphs
support target prioritization, repurposing, mechanism analysis, safety reasoning, and evidence
integration across heterogeneous sources.  Graph-based reasoning can answer multi-hop questions,
such as connecting a compound to a target, the target to a pathway, and the pathway to a disease
phenotype. However, a path in a graph does not prove causality or clinical efficacy. Edge
provenance, confidence, directionality, and biological context matter.  An agent should use
knowledge graphs as structured evidence, not as automatic truth. Good explanations should cite the
relationship chain and indicate whether the evidence is experimental, curated, predicted, or
inferred.
