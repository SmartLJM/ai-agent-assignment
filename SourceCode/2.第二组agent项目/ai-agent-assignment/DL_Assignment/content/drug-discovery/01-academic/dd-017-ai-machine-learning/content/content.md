# AI and Machine Learning in Drug Discovery

**Knowledge set ID:** `dd-017-ai-machine-learning`  
**Theme:** `drug-discovery`  
**Source:** Artificial intelligence in drug discovery: recent advances and future perspectives  

## Curated Content

Artificial intelligence supports drug discovery by learning patterns from chemical, biological,
clinical, imaging, and literature data. Applications include target identification, bioactivity
prediction, virtual screening, de novo molecular generation, synthesis planning, ADMET prediction,
toxicity prediction, drug repurposing, and scientific information extraction.  Deep learning models
can discover nonlinear relationships that are difficult to encode manually. However, model
performance depends on data quality, task definition, train-test splitting, bias control,
applicability domain, and prospective validation. A model that performs well on retrospective
benchmarks may fail on new chemical series or new biology.  Responsible use of AI requires
uncertainty estimation, interpretability where possible, experimental feedback, and pharmacological
reasoning. A useful answer should frame AI as a decision-support technology rather than a
replacement for medicinal chemistry, biology, and clinical evidence.
