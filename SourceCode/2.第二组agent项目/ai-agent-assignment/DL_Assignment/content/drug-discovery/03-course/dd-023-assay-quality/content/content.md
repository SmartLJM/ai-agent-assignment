# Assay Quality and Screening Statistics

**Knowledge set ID:** `dd-023-assay-quality`  
**Theme:** `drug-discovery`  
**Source:** Assay Guidance Manual: Assay Operations for SAR Support  

## Curated Content

Assay quality determines whether screening results are interpretable. A screening assay should have
relevant biology, stable reagents, robust signal, acceptable variability, and suitable positive and
negative controls. Poor assays generate misleading hit lists regardless of how advanced the
downstream computational method is.  Screening statistics such as signal-to-background, signal-to-
noise, coefficient of variation, and Z-factor help evaluate assay performance. Orthogonal assays and
counter-screens are used to identify artifacts such as fluorescence interference, aggregation,
cytotoxicity, nonspecific reactivity, or reporter-system effects.  An agent should connect hit
discovery to assay quality. A molecule is not a reliable hit merely because it appears active in one
primary assay; reproducibility and artifact control are essential.
