# Lead Identification

**Knowledge set ID:** `dd-004-lead-identification`  
**Theme:** `drug-discovery`  
**Source:** Principles of early drug discovery  

## Curated Content

Lead identification converts confirmed hits into more reliable chemical starting points. A lead
should have enough potency, selectivity, chemical tractability, and preliminary drug-like properties
to justify systematic optimization. Lead identification usually combines confirmatory assays,
orthogonal assays, counter-screens, preliminary ADME testing, and early structure-activity
relationship analysis.  The goal is not to create the final medicine immediately. Instead, it is to
identify a compound series that can be improved through medicinal chemistry. Multiple hit series may
be compared by potency, novelty, synthetic feasibility, selectivity against related targets,
solubility, metabolic stability, and early toxicity signals.  In computational settings, lead
identification can use docking, pharmacophore searching, similarity search, QSAR models, active
learning, and multi-objective ranking. A correct answer should state that a lead is stronger than a
raw hit but still requires optimization before preclinical candidate selection.
