# Toxicology and Safety Screening

**Knowledge set ID:** `dd-011-toxicology-safety`  
**Theme:** `drug-discovery`  
**Source:** ICH Safety Guidelines  

## Curated Content

Toxicology and safety screening evaluate whether a compound may cause harmful biological effects.
Early screening can include cytotoxicity, genotoxicity, hERG channel inhibition, mitochondrial
toxicity, reactive metabolite risk, liver toxicity, and off-target pharmacology. Later safety work
includes animal toxicology, safety pharmacology, dose-range finding, and risk assessment.  Safety
must be interpreted together with exposure and mechanism. A hazard observed only at very high
concentrations may have a different implication from toxicity near the expected therapeutic
exposure. Mechanism-based safety concerns are especially important when the target or pathway is
also needed in healthy tissues.  AI models can flag potential toxicity, but a toxicity alert is not
the same as confirmed clinical risk. A useful agent answer should connect the type of safety signal,
the possible mechanism, and the need for experimental confirmation.
