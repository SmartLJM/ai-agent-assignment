# Target Validation

**Knowledge set ID:** `dd-002-target-validation`  
**Theme:** `drug-discovery`  
**Source:** NCATS Drug Discovery and Development  

## Curated Content

Target validation tests whether modulating a chosen target produces the desired therapeutic effect
in relevant biological systems. It reduces the risk that a candidate target is only correlated with
disease rather than causally involved. Validation may use CRISPR knockout, CRISPR activation or
inhibition, RNA interference, chemical probes, antibodies, animal models, patient-derived cells,
organoids, or rescue experiments.  Good validation asks whether target modulation changes disease-
relevant biomarkers or phenotypes at a level that could plausibly translate to patients. It also
examines tissue specificity, compensatory pathways, and whether inhibition or activation is the
correct therapeutic direction. A target can be genetically associated with a disease but still fail
validation if modulation is not feasible, not selective, or creates unacceptable safety risks.  In
an AI agent system, target validation questions often require retrieval from biological evidence,
assay data, and safety knowledge. The answer should distinguish evidence strength from mere
prediction, because target validation is an experimental and mechanistic claim, not only a model
score.
