# Clinical Trial Phases

**Knowledge set ID:** `dd-015-clinical-trial-phases`  
**Theme:** `drug-discovery`  
**Source:** The Drug Development Process: Step 3 Clinical Research  

## Curated Content

Clinical trials evaluate investigational drugs in humans through staged phases. Phase 1 usually
studies safety, tolerability, dosage, and pharmacokinetics in a small group of participants. Phase 2
explores efficacy and side effects in patients with the target condition and helps select dose and
endpoints. Phase 3 tests efficacy and safety in larger populations to support regulatory decisions.
Some programs also include Phase 0 exploratory microdosing studies or Phase 4 post-marketing studies
after approval. Trial design depends on disease area, risk, endpoint type, unmet need, and
regulatory requirements. Randomization, blinding, control groups, inclusion criteria, and
statistical power are central design considerations.  The agent should avoid saying that a drug is
approved simply because it enters clinical trials. Clinical development is evidence generation under
ethical and regulatory oversight.
