# Hit Discovery and High-Throughput Screening

**Knowledge set ID:** `dd-003-hit-discovery-hts`  
**Theme:** `drug-discovery`  
**Source:** Assay Guidance Manual  

## Curated Content

Hit discovery is the process of finding initial molecules that show measurable activity against a
target, pathway, or phenotype. High-throughput screening uses automated liquid handling, plate
readers, robotic systems, and miniaturized assays to test thousands to millions of compounds.
Primary screening produces candidate hits, but these hits require confirmation because false
positives are common.  A robust screening campaign depends on assay quality, controls,
reproducibility, and appropriate statistics. Common measures include signal window, background,
positive and negative controls, concentration-response behavior, and orthogonal confirmation assays.
Compounds that interfere with detection technology, aggregate, react nonspecifically, or show assay
artifacts should be removed before lead identification.  For Q&A systems, hit discovery differs from
lead optimization. A hit is an early signal of activity; it is not yet a drug candidate. The agent
should explain the need for retesting, counter-screening, and prioritization before treating hits as
chemical starting points.
