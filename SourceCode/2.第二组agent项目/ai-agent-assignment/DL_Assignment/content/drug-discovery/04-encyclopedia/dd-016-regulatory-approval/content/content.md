# Regulatory Approval

**Knowledge set ID:** `dd-016-regulatory-approval`  
**Theme:** `drug-discovery`  
**Source:** The Drug Development Process  

## Curated Content

Regulatory approval requires evidence that a drug is safe, effective, and manufactured with
consistent quality for its intended use. Agencies review preclinical data, clinical efficacy,
clinical safety, pharmacology, labeling, risk management, and chemistry, manufacturing, and
controls. The decision is a benefit-risk judgment for a defined population and indication.  Approval
is not based only on molecular novelty or computational prediction. It requires controlled evidence
and documentation that the product can be produced reliably. Regulators may request additional
studies, labeling restrictions, post-marketing commitments, or risk mitigation strategies.  A
correct answer should connect approval to evidence standards. A candidate molecule from AI screening
or docking remains a hypothesis until validated through the required preclinical, clinical, and
manufacturing pathway.
