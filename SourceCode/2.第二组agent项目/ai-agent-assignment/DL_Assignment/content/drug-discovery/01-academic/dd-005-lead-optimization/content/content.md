# Lead Optimization

**Knowledge set ID:** `dd-005-lead-optimization`  
**Theme:** `drug-discovery`  
**Source:** How to improve R&D productivity: the pharmaceutical industry’s grand challenge  

## Curated Content

Lead optimization is the iterative improvement of a lead series through design, synthesis, testing,
and analysis. The aim is to balance potency, selectivity, solubility, permeability, metabolic
stability, safety, pharmacokinetics, and manufacturability. A compound that is very potent but
insoluble, unstable, or toxic may fail as a development candidate.  Medicinal chemists use
structure-activity relationships to understand how chemical changes affect biological and
physicochemical properties. Computational tools can support lead optimization by predicting binding
poses, ADMET liabilities, off-target risks, synthetic accessibility, and property trade-offs. Multi-
objective optimization is important because improving one property can worsen another.  A successful
optimization campaign nominates a preclinical candidate with a justified target product profile and
acceptable risk. In agentic Q&A, lead optimization answers should therefore mention iterative
decision-making, not only potency improvement.
