# Drug Repurposing

**Knowledge set ID:** `dd-014-drug-repurposing`  
**Theme:** `drug-discovery`  
**Source:** Drug repurposing: progress, challenges and recommendations  

## Curated Content

Drug repurposing identifies new therapeutic uses for approved or previously investigated drugs. It
can reduce development risk because some information about safety, formulation, pharmacokinetics, or
manufacturing may already exist. Repurposing strategies include target-based reasoning, disease
similarity, transcriptomic reversal, electronic health record mining, network medicine, and
phenotypic screening.  Repurposing is not automatically easy. The new indication may require
different dosing, different exposure, new efficacy evidence, patent or commercial strategy, and
safety evaluation for a new patient population. A drug safe in one context may be unsafe or
ineffective in another.  AI can help generate repurposing hypotheses, but clinical evidence is still
required. A high-quality answer should explain both the potential advantage and the need for
mechanistic and clinical validation.
