# Generative and De Novo Molecular Design

**Knowledge set ID:** `dd-022-generative-de-novo-design`  
**Theme:** `drug-discovery`  
**Source:** Molecular generative models for drug discovery  

## Curated Content

Generative and de novo molecular design uses algorithms to propose new chemical structures with
desired properties. Approaches include recurrent neural networks, variational autoencoders,
generative adversarial networks, diffusion models, graph generative models, transformers, and
reinforcement learning. These models can explore chemical space beyond existing screening libraries.
The challenge is that a generated molecule must be valid, synthesizable, novel enough to matter, and
optimized across multiple objectives such as potency, selectivity, ADME, toxicity, and intellectual
property. Optimizing only a predicted score can produce unrealistic or artifact-driven compounds.
In scientific Q&A, de novo design should be described as candidate generation, not drug invention by
itself. Generated compounds require synthesis planning, experimental testing, and safety evaluation
before they can become leads.
